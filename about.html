John Reardon

Father, husband, son, brother, friend. Everything that makes up a full and imperfect life.

Before

Australian Media, Marketing, and Advertising. Running a small business. Working with major brands, senior decision-makers, government stakeholders. Fast paced, ambitious, competitive. In hindsight, far more human than I knew at the time.

Shift

Grief, burnout, becoming a father, and reassessing what "success" actually meant slowly shifted my focus away from achievement and toward clarity. Eventually, I realized I wanted to work directly with people and draw on the practical skills developed through business, leadership, and life experience.

2022 onwards

Master of Social Work. Worked within a Community Information and Support Service, supporting individuals and families experiencing financial hardship. Grounding, confronting at times, deeply instructive.

"Time by itself doesn't necessarily define good practice in a field this human. For me, good work is defined by reflection, integrity, and accountability to the people I sit with."

The missing middle

What continues to shape my approach is the growing number of people who fall into what could be described as the "missing middle." They are not in crisis, but they also recognize that something in their life needs attention. They are functioning, capable, and responsible, yet aware that continuing on autopilot may eventually lead somewhere they don't want to end up.

Support for this space often sits outside traditional systems. That is the space this work aims to sit within. Clear, grounded conversations that help people step back, gain perspective, and move forward with intention.

In an ideal world, we do meaningful work together, and you leave with clarity, confidence, and enough momentum to continue building the life you want on your own. That's the goal.
